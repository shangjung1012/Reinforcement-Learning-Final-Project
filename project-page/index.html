Problem

Route retrieval instead of fixing one retriever.

A single retrieval action is rarely optimal for every query. SciFact benefits from hybrid and rewrite-heavy behavior, while NFCorpus often stays competitive with one-call BM25 or dense retrieval. We frame this as an offline contextual-bandit problem over retrieval actions, using logged retrieval outcomes as training and evaluation evidence.

The evaluation is intentionally focused on retrieval-stage evidence quality. Reward combines retrieval metrics and a retrieval-call penalty, so the learned policy must trade off quality gains against extra calls instead of only maximizing recall.

Method

Offline routing policy

State

Query and retrieval-score features, including BM25/dense gaps and score-shape signals.

Actions

BM25, dense, hybrid, and keyword-rewrite variants evaluated as retrieval actions; generated-query actions are optional expensive extensions.

Learning

Selected-action bandit baselines and a selective learned policy trained from logged outcomes.

Objective

Reward and constrained utility with retrieval-call cost, reported against the train-best action.

Evidence boundary

HotpotQA shows a positive answer-quality pilot for the learned policy. NQ has the same EM across methods and mixed F1, so downstream answer improvement remains a pilot finding.

Diagnostics

What the result does and does not claim

Where routing helps

High action-disagreement slices show larger gains: +0.049 on SciFact and +0.096 on NFCorpus.

Robustness

Across three seeds, selective policy win rate is 0.667 on both datasets, with mean deltas of +0.021 and +0.025.

OPE caveat

IPS error increases under sparse train-best logging on SciFact, so we emphasize direct held-out replay and artifact checks.

Scope

This page's final claim is retrieval evidence quality. Gemini reader EM/F1 is shown separately as an API pilot, not an end-to-end answer-generation benchmark.
